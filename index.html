Introducing: DAWN



Water scarcity is becoming increasingly prevalent, especially for residents of mobile homes and tiny homes, which often lack water infrastructure. Existing rainwater capture solutions are dependent on homes having gutters and large roofs, and need to be permanently and professionally installed. This makes them not accessible for mobile and tiny homes. DAWN can be easily installed by a consumer, is completely free-standing, increases water capture area, and is easily collapsible. Additionally, customers that move can simply pack it up and reinstall the system at their next location.

The System

DAWN includes a deployable awning, collapsible rain barrel, and an off-the-shelf filtration system. The awning expands the water catchment area of the tiny home, guiding the water through an off-the-shelf filter to a water barrel where it is stored for future use.

The Awning

The awning is made of a metal frame and a double-layer fabric cover, all of which was made in-house. It is completely free-standing and requires no profession installation, making it cheap and easy to install on any type of home. The awning can be retracted or expanded by hand so it doesn't take up space when not in use.

The Barrel

The barrel is made of an aluminum and wood frame that supports the double-layer fabric bag which contains the water. It was made entirely in-house. The barrel stores the rainwater that is captured by the awning. It is easy to assemble, and has 3 openings: an attachable inlet pipe at the top, a spigot outlet at the bottom, and a dry bag opening at the top for cleaning.

Awning Details

  • Jump to Barrel

Harvests Rainwater!

The awning expands the water catchment area of the building. The awning has a lip on each side to prevent water from falling off, and it is tilted to guide water towards the exit pipe. This was tested by pouring water on the awning. The awning can survive up to 25 mph winds as required by regulations.

Easy to Assemble!

The awning is very easy to assemble. To get around needing professional installation or a specific mounting point, the awning base is anchored to the ground using ground screws. The awning is assembled by hand on the ground, then mounted to the ground screws to stand it up.

Foldable Arms!

The awning isn't just a permanent structure that captures water - a lot of residents don't want the area around their homes permanently covered. Instead, it can be expanded and retracted like a normal collapsible awning. When retracted, it takes up almost no space. Additionally, the awning can be easily dissambled and stored for long-term storage or moving.

Barrel Details

  • Jump to Overview
  • Jump to Awning

Stores Rainwater!

The barrel stores the rainwater captured by the awning. It is easy to assemble by hand and can be flat-packed away like IKEA furniture The barrel was tested for leaking, but was watertight even when shaken upside down. The base of the barrel was analyzed with an FEA and passed with a high FOS.

Easy Assembly!

There are two main parts of the barrel. The bag holds the water and is sewen out of a double layered fabric: a Nylon Ripstop interior for food safety and a Marine Vinyl exterior for strength. The structure gives the bag a solid foundation and support. The sloped bottom guides water to the outlet.

Integrated Piping!

The inlet pipe is attached by inserting it through the hole and screwing a ring onto the inside to prevent leaking. The outlet stays connected at all times, but the raised base of the barrel allows you to easily attach a pipe below the valve. The entry and exit valves are compliant with ANSI 61 Drinking Water Standards. The top of the barrel opens like a dry bag opening for easy cleaning.
